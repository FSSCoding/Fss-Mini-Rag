# Workplace Compliance Manual

## Section 1: Anti-Discrimination Policy

All employees are protected from discrimination based on race, gender, age, disability, religion, sexual orientation, or national origin. This policy applies to all aspects of employment including hiring, promotion, compensation, and termination.

Complaints should be filed with the HR department within 30 days of the incident. Anonymous reporting is available through the ethics hotline at 1-800-555-0199.

Retaliation against employees who file complaints is strictly prohibited and will result in immediate disciplinary action up to and including termination.

## Section 2: Workplace Safety Standards

All employees must complete safety orientation within the first week of employment. This includes:
- Fire evacuation procedures and assembly points
- First aid kit locations and AED training
- Personal protective equipment requirements
- Chemical handling and MSDS documentation
- Ergonomic workstation setup guidelines

Safety inspections are conducted monthly by the facilities team. Any hazards must be reported immediately using the incident reporting system. Near-miss events must also be documented.

Emergency exits must remain unobstructed at all times. Fire drills are conducted quarterly.

## Section 3: Data Protection and Privacy

All customer data must be handled in accordance with GDPR and local privacy laws. Personally identifiable information (PII) includes names, addresses, email addresses, phone numbers, social security numbers, and financial data.

Data classification levels:
- **Public**: Marketing materials, press releases
- **Internal**: Company policies, org charts
- **Confidential**: Customer data, financial reports
- **Restricted**: Trade secrets, encryption keys, access credentials

Data retention periods:
- Customer transaction data: 7 years
- Employee records: Duration of employment plus 6 years
- Email communications: 3 years
- Security camera footage: 90 days

Data breach notification must occur within 72 hours of discovery.

## Section 4: Code of Conduct

Employees are expected to maintain professional conduct at all times. This includes:
- Honest and transparent communication
- Respect for colleagues and customers
- Protection of company assets and intellectual property
- Compliance with all applicable laws and regulations
- Avoidance of conflicts of interest

Gift acceptance policy: Employees may not accept gifts valued at more than $50 from vendors or clients. All gifts must be reported to the compliance department.

Social media policy: Employees must not disclose confidential company information on social media platforms. Personal social media accounts must include a disclaimer that views expressed are personal and do not represent the company.

## Section 5: Financial Controls

All expenditures over $500 require manager approval. Expenditures over $5,000 require director approval. Expenditures over $25,000 require VP approval.

Expense reports must be submitted within 30 days of incurrence. Receipts are required for all expenses over $25. Corporate credit card statements must be reconciled monthly.

Travel policy:
- Economy class for flights under 6 hours
- Business class requires VP pre-approval
- Hotel rates must not exceed GSA per diem rates
- Meal expenses capped at $75 per day domestic, $100 international

## Section 6: Whistleblower Protection

The company maintains a zero-tolerance policy for retaliation against whistleblowers. Employees who report suspected fraud, waste, abuse, or violations of law or policy in good faith are protected under this policy.

Reports can be made to:
- Direct supervisor
- HR department
- Legal department
- Anonymous ethics hotline: 1-800-555-0199
- Online portal: ethics.company.com

Investigation timeline: All reports will be acknowledged within 48 hours and investigated within 30 business days. The reporter will be notified of the outcome unless anonymous.

## Section 7: Environmental Compliance

The company is committed to environmental sustainability and compliance with all EPA regulations. This includes:
- Proper disposal of hazardous waste through licensed contractors
- Annual emissions reporting and reduction targets
- Water usage monitoring and conservation programs
- Energy efficiency standards for all facilities
- Recycling programs for paper, plastic, electronics, and batteries

Environmental incidents must be reported to the safety department within 1 hour. Spills of reportable quantities must also be reported to the EPA National Response Center.

Carbon footprint reduction target: 30% reduction by 2030 relative to 2020 baseline.
